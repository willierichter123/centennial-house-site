Fall in St. Augustine: A Quieter, Better Way to See the City

Oct 3, 2025 Carriage Way Centennial House

Spring gets the postcards, but fall — specifically October and early November — might actually be the city’s best stretch. Cooler mornings, smaller crowds, and the same beautiful streets.

The weather is finally human

After a long, heavy summer, October arrives with low 80s and dry mornings. By early November you can leave the windows open. It’s a noticeable, full-body relief if you’ve spent a Florida summer.

Crowds drop

Schools are back, summer travelers are gone, and you’re in a brief window before the holiday lights bring everyone back in late November. Restaurants are easier, tours are smaller, the city feels like a city instead of a destination.

Nights of Lights builds quietly

By mid-November the historic district begins lighting up for the season. The full event runs through January, but the soft, partial version in November — before the crowds — is the one we’d send a returning guest to.

Our recommendation

If you’ve only ever visited in spring or summer, book three nights in late October. You won’t see a different city, but you’ll see the city in a different mood. It’s our personal favorite.
