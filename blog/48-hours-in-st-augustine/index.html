How to Spend 48 Hours in the Nation’s Oldest City

Jul 4, 2025 Carriage Way Centennial House

St. Augustine rewards slow travel. This is a relaxed two-day plan that covers the highlights without feeling rushed — the version we’d hand a friend.

Day one: get oriented

Start at the Castillo — it’s the easiest geographical anchor and the best way to understand the city’s layout. From there, walk the bayfront south to the plaza, then loop back through the smaller streets. By dinner you’ll know roughly where things are.

Day one evening

Dinner somewhere unhurried. We’d send you to one of the smaller spots on Hypolita or the Ice Plant across the bridge. Walk the plaza after — the lighting after dark is part of the appeal.

Day two: go a little deeper

After breakfast, choose one museum (the Lightner is our pick) and one outdoor stretch (Anastasia State Park or a trolley out to the lighthouse). Lunch back in the historic district. Save the afternoon for nothing — a porch chair, a book, a slow walk.

Day two evening

If you’ve still got energy, a sunset on the bayfront, then a casual dinner. If you don’t, room service from a takeout spot eaten on the porch is a perfectly defensible final evening.

What we’d skip

Honestly — the trolley narration repeats. The Fountain of Youth is a curiosity but doesn’t need to be a stop on a 48-hour trip. And if you’re tempted by an attraction with the word ‘world’ in its name, consider it carefully.
