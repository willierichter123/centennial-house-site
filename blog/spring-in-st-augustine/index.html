5 Reasons Spring Is the Best Time to Visit St. Augustine

Apr 10, 2026 Carriage Way Centennial House

By the time the dogwoods bloom along Cordova Street, we’re already in our favorite stretch of the year. Spring in St. Augustine is short, generous, and almost embarrassingly photogenic — here’s why we tell guests to plan around it.

1. The weather actually cooperates

Mornings hover in the upper 60s, afternoons settle in the high 70s, and the humidity that makes August feel like soup hasn’t shown up yet. You can walk the historic district at 2 p.m. without changing shirts — that’s the magic.

2. Crowds taper off after spring break

Once the first week of April passes, foot traffic on St. George Street thins to something resembling normal life. You can sit at the bar at Columbia, walk into the Castillo without a line, and find a porch table at breakfast without booking three days ahead.

3. The historic district is in full bloom

The bougainvillea on Aviles Street, the wisteria draped over a few of the older walls, the jasmine that quietly takes over a block at a time — everything is happening at once. Bring a camera you actually like using.

4. The water finally warms up

Anastasia State Park is reachable by trolley or a short drive, and the Atlantic by mid-April is past the chilly stretch. It’s a beach day with sweater-weather mornings, which is the best version of any beach day.

5. Our porch is unbeatable in April

We may be biased, but our front porch in spring is one of the better places to spend a slow afternoon in the city. Coffee in the morning, a book in the late afternoon, neighbors waving as they walk by. That’s the trip.
