The History Behind 26 Cordova Street

Nov 7, 2025 Carriage Way Centennial House

Our address has changed hands, names, and purposes more times than we can confidently list. Here’s the version of the story we’ve been able to verify — and the parts that are still a bit of a mystery.

A late-1800s residence

The original structure went up in the 1880s as a private residence during the Henry Flagler era, when St. Augustine was being reinvented as a winter destination for the northeast wealthy. The wood frame, the wraparound porch, and most of the interior trim are original to that period.

Decades as a guesthouse

By the early 1900s, like many of the larger homes in the district, it had been converted into a guesthouse. Records are spotty, but we know it operated under at least three different names through the 20th century.

What we’ve preserved

When we took the building on, we made a decision early: don’t modernize the bones. The heart pine floors, the original windows, the quirks of a late-Victorian floor plan — all kept. The systems behind the walls are new. The walls themselves aren’t.

What’s still uncertain

We’d love to know more about the families who lived here in the early years. If you’re reading this and you have ties to the property — a relative, a photograph, a story — we’d genuinely like to hear from you.
