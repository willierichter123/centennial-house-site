The Castillo de San Marcos: Everything First-Time Visitors Should Know

Feb 14, 2026 Carriage Way Centennial House

It’s the oldest masonry fort in the continental U.S., and it’s a five-minute walk from our front door. Here’s how to make the visit feel like more than a checked box.

Go early

Gates open at 9 a.m. and the first ninety minutes are the best part of the day to be there — soft light, fewer people, and the bay is usually flat. By eleven the tour groups arrive.

The cannon firing

Reenactors fire the cannons several times most weekends. Check the schedule at the visitor desk when you arrive. It’s loud and worth waiting for.

What to look for

The fort is built of coquina — a soft shellstone that absorbed cannon fire instead of shattering, which is partly why this place is still standing after three centuries of attacks. The chapel, the powder magazine, and the upper deck overlooking the inlet are the three stops we’d prioritize.

Practical notes

Tickets are around $15 and good for seven days. Wear sun protection — the upper terrace has none. Allow ninety minutes minimum, two hours if you stop to read the displays.
