Why Couples Keep Coming Back to St. Augustine

Jan 30, 2026 Carriage Way Centennial House

We see the same names in our reservations every year. Anniversaries, birthdays, the third trip in four years — we asked a few of our regulars why St. Augustine is the place they keep choosing.

It’s walkable in a way most cities aren’t

The whole historic district is half a mile across. You can park the car on Friday and not touch it again until Sunday. That kind of weekend — no driving, no parking, no app — is harder to find than people realize.

It’s old without trying

The architecture is genuinely four centuries old, the streets predate the country, and the Spanish moss on the live oaks isn’t a stage set. It rewards travelers who like to feel a place’s age.

The pace fits a real vacation

Couples tell us this one over and over: the city doesn’t demand anything. There’s no must-do list that’ll ruin the trip if you skip it. You can sleep in, take a long breakfast, walk the bayfront, and have done the day correctly.

And the food has caught up

Five years ago we’d have said the dining was uneven. Now there’s a generation of chefs working out of small spaces in the historic district doing genuinely careful cooking. The restaurants list on our blog is updated quarterly — the latest version is worth reading before you book.
