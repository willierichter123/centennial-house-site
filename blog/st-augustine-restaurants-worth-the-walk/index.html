St. Augustine Restaurants Worth the Walk

Aug 21, 2025 Carriage Way Centennial House

All of these are within walking distance of the inn. All of them have been recommended by guests or vetted by us. A short, opinionated list — not the longest, but the one we actually stand behind.

For a special evening

Collage on Hypolita Street is the one we send people to for an anniversary or a quiet milestone dinner. Small dining room, careful kitchen, no spectacle. Reserve a week ahead.

For a casual lunch

The Floridian on Cordova does a Southern-leaning menu with locally sourced everything. We eat here ourselves probably more than we should.

For breakfast outside the inn

The Bunnery on St. George is a long-standing local bakery that does pastries and a strong coffee. If you’ve overslept ours, this is the consolation prize.

For a drink before dinner

The Ice Plant, set in a restored 1927 ice factory across the bridge, has the city’s best cocktail program and a kitchen that takes itself seriously. The walk over the Bridge of Lions is part of the experience.

For dessert

Whetstone Chocolates if you want to bring something home; Mayday on Charlotte Street if you want a single perfect dessert sitting at a counter.
