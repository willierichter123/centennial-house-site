A Walking Guide to the Historic District — From Our Front Porch

Mar 5, 2026 Carriage Way Centennial House

Step off our porch and you’re already in the historic district. This is the route we send guests on when they’ve got a half-day, comfortable shoes, and no particular agenda.

Start: Cordova Street to the City Gate

Head north up Cordova, take a right on Orange Street, and you’ll come out at the old City Gate. Touch the coquina if you like — it’s nearly three hundred years old and locally quarried.

Down St. George Street

St. George is the obvious main artery, and yes, it gets busy. Walk it anyway, but be willing to duck into side streets. The best courtyards and walled gardens aren’t on the main drag.

The Plaza and the Cathedral Basilica

The Plaza de la Constitución is the oldest public space in the country. Sit a minute. The Cathedral Basilica across the street is open to visitors most days — the interior is more striking than the modest façade suggests.

Aviles Street, slowly

If you take one detour, make it Aviles. It’s the oldest street in the U.S., it’s mostly pedestrian, and the galleries and small museums along it are worth the time. Most people miss it.

End: Bayfront and back

Loop back along Avenida Menendez for the bayfront views, the Bridge of Lions, and a clear sightline to the Castillo. You’ll be back at our porch in about an hour and a half if you don’t stop — closer to three if you do (and you should).
