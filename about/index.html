About

Historic district charm, quieter hospitality.

Descend our porch steps and be transformed into a turn-of-the-century city dweller. Experience all the charm of St. Augustine — no car required.

01 — History

A Historic Neighborhood

Carriage Way Centennial is several blocks away from Flagler College, formerly the Ponce de Leon Hotel, built in 1888. Walk in the footsteps of the once "rich and famous" as you enter the grand rotunda, or visit the dining hall showcasing the artistic glasswork of Louis Tiffany.

02 — Explore

Everything Within Reach

The Bridge of Lions, the Lightner Museum, the legendary Fountain of Youth, the Castillo de San Marcos — all just a stroll away. Wander the riverfront of the Intracoastal Waterway where dolphins often appear. Browse St. George Street's galleries and shops, or discover the Spanish Quarter Village and the Oldest House.

03 — Adventure

Adventure Awaits

With the ocean just down the road, adventure is always within reach — from surfing and boating to casting a line or climbing the iconic St. Augustine Lighthouse for breathtaking views. Several excellent golf courses are just around the corner.
